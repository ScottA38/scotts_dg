A 'Jhana' is a meditative state of absorption which achieved through meditation (in Buddhism). Translates to the word 'absorption' in English. Translates as 'Dhyana' in Sanskrit.

There are nine attainable Jhanas in total. The ninth Jhana pertains to the cessation of all mental activity in the mind, complete stillness. 

The preliminary four Jhanas are associated with pleasure and joy. The subsequent four Dhyanas pertain to emphasis on varying aspects of equanimity and tranquility.

The implied state of mental absorption commonly associated with a Dhyana may help the observer in accessing a clearer understanding of principles of reality - specifically the concepts of impermanence and non-self natures. When accessed successfully, the person experiencing a dhyanas may _temporarily_ transcend feelings of greed or jealousy or other emotions which will inhibit our life karmically.
